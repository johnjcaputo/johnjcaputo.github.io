Sometimes you just have to build it yourself

I’ve always been blessed with having a quality instrument to play. But sometimes you just need to get a little more raw and personal. Back to basics, if you would. As an acoustic musician and slide player, sometimes crafiting your own tools is the only way to roll. I’m doubly blessed that my boys like playing around in the garage with me. They’ve touched alot of the builds below.

The Trident

Designed by a 5 year old.

My son wanted a guitar with three necks and five strings. He drew a picture; we build it over Labor Day weekend. It’s mad. It’s awesome. One neck sums up each of us.

I love it because it’s totally Nino in spirit.

Eye of the Tiger

Mileage built in.

I couldn’t turn it my old plate. The milage and the years are all in there. Scraps in the garage plus a few kitties makes a dynamite guitar. The tail piece is a nod to the family.

The Punch Box

My first build.

After the boys and I put together a CB Gitty guitar, I had to design and build one myself. A sweet little box for some late night picking.

Canjo

Yep, it’s a a soup can.

And it was delicous. Clam chowder, if you care. If not, it’s okay because it sounds awesome. Another plus is the boys love grabbing it and singing their favorite song while striking some crazy rock star poses. Playing it with a stick reminds me of Tony Levin’s funk fingers.

Diddley Bow

An electric stick.

The next build after the canjo. I wanted a live stick to cover G and A and this fit the bill. Surprisingly soulful with a a new take on vibrato for me; more of a bend than a traditional slide vibrato.

Kalimbas

Like a handpan, only obtainable.

Love playing these. The closest thing to a hand pan I could get to before finding the Hapi Drum. Still love my kalimbas though. Solid or cigar box with a pickup, they’re all good. Tip of the hat to my father-in-law for some of the engineering and jigs for these guys.
