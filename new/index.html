music

Ableton when it needs to be grand; just me and my ukulele when it needs to be intimate. I love the groove, warm breezes, and just letting Music go where it wants to.

Sometimes I make my own instruments too.

Enjoy.

Canjo
Yep, it’s a soup can. Clam chowder; it was delicous. The boys love grabbing it and singing their favorite song while striking some crazy rock star poses. Playing it with a stick reminds me of Tony Levin’s funk fingers.
Electric Diddley Bow
An electric stick. The next build after the canjo. I wanted a live stick to cover G and A and this fits the bill. Surprisingly soulful.
Kalimbas
Like a handpan, only obtainable. Love playing these. Solid or cigar box with a pickup, they’re all good. Tip of the hat to my father-in-law for some of the engineering and jigs for these guys.
The Tigers
Mileage built in. I couldn’t turn in my old plate. The milage and the years are all in there. Scraps in the garage plus a few tuners makes a dynamite guitar. Tuned to C for some barritone goodness.
Punch Box
My first guitar build. After the boys and I put together a CB Gitty guitar, I had to design and build one myself. A sweet little box for some late night picking. Currently residing in a new home.
The Trident
Designed by a 5 year old. My son wanted a guitar with three necks and five strings. He drew a picture; we built it over Labor Day weekend. It’s mad. It’s awesome; and totally my son in spirit.

me

Musician, adventurer, photographer, and learning designer/technolgist from the Philadelphia suburbs. I moonlight as an adjunct professor. If that doesn't cover enough ground, I also pretend that I know what I'm doing as a parent and husband.

stuff I play: Electric and acoustic guitar (6, 7, 8 and 12 string), cigar box guitars, diddley bows, canjos, bass, piano, lap steel, resonator, tenor banjo, ukulele, kalimba, Zendrum, Hapi drum, djembe, Midi Fighters, Push, and anything that I can make a sound with (and not necessarily in that order).

stuff I make: Kalimbas, guitars with cigar boxes and license plates, Legos, whatever odds & ends I need, and a colossal mess (which I clean up).
